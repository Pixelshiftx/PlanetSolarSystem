Jupiter

Jupiter is, by far, the largest planet in the solar system – more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined. Jupiter's familiar stripes and swirls are actually cold, windy clouds of ammonia and water, floating in an atmosphere of hydrogen and helium. It was named after Jupiter, the chief deity of ancient Roman religion.

Jupiter is surrounded by a faint planetary ring system and a powerful magnetosphere. The planet's magnetic tail is nearly 800 million kilometres long, covering nearly the entire distance to Saturn's orbit. Jupiter has 84 known moons and likely many more, including the four large moons discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
